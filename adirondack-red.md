# Adirondack Red

## Summary

Adirondack Red is an early to mid-season potato variety bred by Cornell University and released in 2004. Prized for its distinctive red-purple skin and pink-red flesh, it is a specialty market variety notable for its high antioxidant content, particularly anthocyanins. It offers medium to high yields and is valued for roasting, baking, and frying applications.

## Agronomic notes

Adirondack Red matures in 80–100 days after sowing, making it an early to mid-season variety. Tubers are medium-sized, oblong and slightly flattened with a distinctive pink-red skin featuring light netting and shallow eyes. The flesh is pink-red, often speckled with white. The variety delivers high yields with a specific gravity of 1.0675, indicating good dry-matter content suitable for multiple culinary applications.

The plant exhibits a semi-upright growth habit with dark green, spreading vines. Leaves are medium green with intermediate foliage silhouette, and flowers are few but distinctive—purple-red in colour with yellow-red anthers.

Strengths include moderate resistance to common scab and excellent resistance to hollow heart and internal defects. Notably, Adirondack Red contains high levels of antioxidants (particularly anthocyanins at approximately 14–15 mg per 100 g fresh weight), giving it 2–3 times higher total antioxidant capacity than unpigmented varieties. Weaknesses include susceptibility to silver scurf, rhizoctonia, growth cracks, golden cyst nematode, pink rot, and Fusarium.

## Culinary notes

Adirondack Red is prized by chefs and home cooks as a specialty table potato. Its vibrant red-purple skin and pink-red flesh make it visually distinctive in dishes. The high dry-matter content (specific gravity 1.0675) makes it well-suited to roasting, baking, and frying—it holds its shape and develops good colour when cooked. The variety is also valued for its nutritional profile, with exceptionally high antioxidant levels, making it a choice for health-conscious cooking. It is marketed as a specialty ingredient in upscale restaurants and farmers' markets.

## Origin & history

Adirondack Red was bred at Cornell University by potato breeders Robert Plaisted, Ken Paddock, and Walter De Jong. The variety resulted from a cross made in early 1994 between N40-2 (itself a progeny of Chieftain × Black Russian) and Q155-3. It was initially designated T17-2 and underwent evaluation and refinement before being officially named Adirondack Red and released in 2004. The variety is part of the Adirondack family of coloured potatoes, all notable for their pigmented skin and flesh and exceptional antioxidant profiles. It represents the product of deliberate breeding for nutrition and ornamental appeal in addition to agronomic performance.
