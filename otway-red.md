# Otway Red

## Summary

Otway Red is an Australian-bred potato developed by the Bone family in Victoria's Otway Ranges. It is a popular, high-yielding variety with smooth dark-red skin and creamy white flesh, producing oval tubers. Prized for its excellent earthy flavour and thick skin, which makes it an outstanding storage potato.

## Agronomic notes

Otway Red is a high-yielding variety that matures in 60–90 days after the last frost, making it suitable for early-to-maincrop growing seasons. The tubers are medium-sized and oval in form with smooth, dark-red skin. The variety produces thick-skinned potatoes that store exceptionally well, retaining quality over extended storage periods. Growing information indicates the variety can be planted from autumn to spring across Australian growing regions, with harvest times of 16–18 weeks depending on planting date.

## Culinary notes

Otway Red is an all-purpose potato with excellent versatility in the kitchen. Its creamy white flesh suits roasting, mashing, baking, and chip production equally well. The variety is known for its outstanding earthy flavour and can be prepared using nearly any cooking method. It is particularly valued for salads and boiling dishes where its waxy texture helps it retain shape. The thick skin and firm structure make it ideal for roasting, and its flavour profile complements both simple preparations and complex dishes.

## Origin & history

Otway Red was developed by the Bone family in the Otway Ranges of Victoria, Australia, making it a locally bred variety with regional significance. The precise year of introduction is not documented in available sources, but the variety has become established as a popular Australian cultivar. The potato is named after its region of origin, the Otway Ranges, reflecting its Victorian heritage.
