# Coliban

## Summary
Coliban is a white, all-purpose potato variety originating from the United Kingdom but now most commonly associated with Australian cultivation. Its medium starch content and firm texture make it versatile in the kitchen, suitable for both everyday boiling and more elaborate roasting or frying applications. It has become one of Australia's most widely grown and commercially significant white potato varieties.

## Agronomic notes
Coliban is an early-maincrop variety that typically produces medium-sized, round to oval tubers with smooth, creamy white skin and white flesh. The variety is known for relatively high yields and good disease resistance, making it an attractive cultivar for commercial production. It demonstrates excellent tolerance to a range of climate conditions, which has enabled it to become the dominant white potato variety in Australian markets. The variety is easy to grow and store, and maintains good shape during cooking.

## Culinary notes
Coliban potatoes have medium starch content and a firm texture that holds well during cooking, making them excellent for boiling and salads. The mild flavour profile makes them suitable for a wide range of dishes. They are particularly valued for mashing, baking, and roasting, where their waxy-to-all-purpose nature prevents them from becoming too fluffy. Coliban is also commonly used for chip and fries production. As a generic white potato, it has become a staple in Australian supermarkets and home kitchens for everyday cooking.

## Origin & history
Coliban was developed in the United Kingdom and released in 1902. The variety was eventually introduced to Australia during the 20th century, where it found ideal growing conditions and became exceptionally popular. Today, it is recognized as one of Australia's primary commercial white potato varieties and is the main generic white potato sold in Australian supermarkets and retail markets including ALDI, Coles, Woolworths, and IGA.
