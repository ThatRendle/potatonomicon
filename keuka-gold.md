# Keuka Gold

## Summary
Keuka Gold is a mid-season yellow potato developed by Cornell University in 2004 to rival the classic Yukon Gold while thriving in the variable climates of the northeastern United States. Its buff skin and light yellow flesh, combined with disease resistance and robust yields, made it immediately popular with organic growers in upstate New York and New England.

## Agronomic notes
Keuka Gold is a vigorous, easy-to-grow variety with white flowers that produces heavy, uniform yields of medium-to-large oval tubers. It matures in 80–90 days as a true mid-season variety and stores well, making it suitable for both fresh market and longer-term storage. The variety is notably resistant to common scab and golden nematode, two significant potato pathogens, which contributed to its success among organic growers. It was specifically bred to perform reliably in the unpredictable weather of the Northeast.

## Culinary notes
Keuka Gold is a waxy, all-purpose yellow potato with a creamy, rich flavour profile almost identical to Yukon Gold. The tubers are at their best when boiled but also excel when roasted or baked. The firm, moist flesh and excellent storability make it a reliable choice for home and market gardens throughout the growing season and into winter storage.

## Origin & history
Keuka Gold was developed by Cornell University's potato breeding program and formally introduced in 2004 as part of the university's Public Seed Initiative. It was bred specifically to create a disease-resistant, high-yielding alternative to Yukon Gold that would perform reliably in the cool, variable climate of the northeastern United States, particularly upstate New York and New England. The variety is named after Keuka Lake in New York's Finger Lakes region, reflecting its regional breeding origin. Its immediate adoption by organic growers speaks to the success of Cornell's breeding objectives.
