# Ruby Lou

## Summary
Ruby Lou is an early to mid-season red-skinned Australian potato bred in Victoria. Prized for its attractive pink skin, creamy white flesh, and uniform crop quality, it is a waxy all-purpose variety beloved by home gardeners and market growers. Its shallow eyes and excellent shape retention make it ideal for boiling, mashing, and salads.

## Agronomic notes
Ruby Lou is a locally bred Victorian cultivar classified as early to mid-season, maturing in approximately 120 days under good conditions. It produces a high and uniform crop of tubers of medium oval shape. The shallow eyes make peeling straightforward. The variety grows best in temperate and cool climates, preferring well-drained, fertile soils with consistent moisture. Regular hilling during growth maximises both yield and tuber quality. Certified seed stock ensures strong plant vigour and reliable resistance to common potato diseases. At optimal conditions, growers can expect yields of up to 10 kg per 1 kg of seed planted.

## Culinary notes
Ruby Lou is a waxy, all-purpose potato prized for its ability to hold its shape well during cooking. The mild flavour and creamy white flesh make it excellent for boiling and mashing. It performs well in cold potato salads, especially when harvested young. The variety microwaves well and produces excellent chips with pale cream colour and crisp exterior. It is also suitable for frying and roasting, though its waxy texture makes it less ideal for baking.

## Origin & history
Ruby Lou is a locally bred Victorian cultivar from Australia. The exact breeder, year of introduction, and parentage are not documented in publicly available sources, but the variety has become a popular choice in Australian home gardens and commercial markets due to its attractive appearance and reliable performance in temperate conditions.
