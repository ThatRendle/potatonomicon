# Desiree

## Summary

Desiree is a red-skinned main-crop potato with light yellow flesh, originally bred in the Netherlands in 1962. It is widely cultivated across the United Kingdom and Australia, and is prized for its high yields, drought resistance, and versatility in the kitchen. Its firm, waxy texture and creamy flavour make it a favourite among gardeners and commercial producers alike.

## Agronomic notes

Desiree is a maincrop variety that reaches maturity in the medium-late season with early bulking characteristics. It produces large, oval tubers with an attractive red skin and produces high yields of 9–11 tubers per plant. The variety has medium dormancy and stores well. It exhibits excellent drought tolerance, making it particularly valuable in drier growing regions.

Disease resistance is a significant strength: Desiree is immune to potato wart and resistant to skin spot, with good resistance to PVY (potato virus Y) and tuber late blight. However, it is susceptible to potato cyst nematodes (Globodera rostochiensis Ro1 and Globodera pallida Pa2/3) and can develop misshapes on heavy soils. It is moderately susceptible to mild mosaic viruses. In wet conditions, it can be prone to late blight, requiring appropriate management.

The variety is sensitive to internal bruising during handling and somewhat sensitive to the herbicide metribuzin, factors worth noting for commercial growers.

## Culinary notes

Desiree is a versatile all-purpose potato with medium-high dry matter content (21.5%) that holds its shape when cooked. Its firm, waxy texture and creamy, distinctive flavour make it excellent for a wide range of applications: smooth mashing (particularly for dishes like Dauphinoise), boiling with butter and herbs, roasting, baking, and salads. It also produces good results for fresh French fries. The variety's ability to maintain structural integrity during cooking makes it especially valued for dishes where presentation matters.

In the UK, it is widely regarded as the most popular maincrop variety for home gardeners and commercial growers. It performs well across most of England and Wales, though it can be watery and less flavourful in Scotland. In Australia, it is commercially cultivated in Tasmania and other cooler regions, where its yield potential and flavour profile are highly valued.

## Origin & history

Desiree was bred in 1951 by ZPC (Zaden Producten Centrum) in Leeuwarden, the Netherlands, from a cross of Urgenta and Depesche. It was officially registered and released in 1962, making it a relatively modern cultivar that emerged during a period of intensive potato breeding in the Netherlands.

The variety rapidly became popular across Europe, particularly in the United Kingdom and Italy, where it has remained a staple maincrop variety for both home and commercial cultivation for over six decades. Its commercial success reflects its combination of agronomic reliability, drought tolerance, and culinary versatility.
