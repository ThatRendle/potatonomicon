# Pink Eye (Southern Gold)

## Summary
Pink Eye is an early-maturing waxy potato grown primarily in Tasmania, Australia, with distinctive cream-yellow skin marked by deep pink or purple eyes. Known for holding its shape well during cooking, it is prized for boiling, salads, and as a fresh "new" potato. The variety became embedded in Tasmanian food culture, particularly for Christmas dinners.

## Agronomic notes
Pink Eye is a first early variety with a maturity window of 70–110 days from planting. Tubers are medium-sized and characteristically round, with cream to yellow skin and deep, prominent pink or purple eyes that give the variety its name. The waxy flesh is creamy yellow. The variety shows good resistance to common scab, a valuable trait for consistent tuber quality. It is grown mainly in southern Tasmania, though seed potato schemes have made it available across Australia. Planted in late August in Tasmania, tubers are ready for harvest by Christmas, making them especially popular for summer holiday meals in the Southern Hemisphere.

## Culinary notes
Pink Eye is a waxy potato that holds its shape excellently after cooking, making it ideal for boiling whole, slicing into salads, and steaming. The creamy, firm texture and waxy flesh are less suited to mashing or frying compared to floury varieties, but it excels in potato salads and as a tender "new" potato boiled in its thin skin. Some cooks use it for baking as well, though this is not its primary strength. The low starch content and high moisture are characteristic of waxy types.

## Origin & history
Pink Eye was first recorded in southern Tasmania in the 1940s and was formally included in the Tasmanian Certified Seed Potato Scheme in 1944. The variety became culturally significant in Tasmania, particularly as a Christmas potato — planted in late August, it matures by December and has become a traditional component of Tasmanian summer Christmas dinners. While its exact breeder or parentage is unknown, the variety spread throughout Tasmania and eventually became available across Australia through certified seed networks. It is sold nation-wide in Australia except in Western Australia.
