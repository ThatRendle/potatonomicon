# Red Norland

## Summary
Red Norland is an early-maturing red-skinned potato with white flesh, widely grown as the standard short-season red in North America. Originally selected as a darker red line from the Norland variety, it is prized for salads and boiling applications due to its low starch content and ability to hold its shape during cooking. The variety is known for rapid emergence, moderate yields, and reliability in northern climates.

## Agronomic notes
Red Norland is an early-season cultivar maturing in 70–90 days after planting with rapid emergence and a determinate growth habit. Tubers are medium-sized, oval to slightly rounded and flattened, with smooth skin and shallow to medium eyes. The variety produces a uniform tuber set with low specific gravity (1.060–1.070), making it excellent for boiling applications. Dormancy is short, enabling rapid regeneration if stored for replanting. Yields are moderate but consistent, and the variety has proven widely adapted across northern regions. It demonstrates moderate resistance to common scab but is susceptible to late blight and common potato viruses, requiring standard disease management in areas with significant late-blight pressure.

## Culinary notes
Red Norland is a waxy potato with low starch content and a mild, slightly sweet flavour, making it ideal for applications where tuber shape retention is critical. It is the standard variety for potato salads, whether served warm or cold, as it absorbs dressings well without breaking apart. Its primary market strength is as a boiling potato, often sold as "new reds" when harvested young; smaller tubers (B and C size) are particularly prized for this purpose. The variety can also be used for frying and chip production, though it is not recommended for baking or mashing due to its low starch and waxy texture, which would produce a dense, gluey result.

## Origin & history
Red Norland is a selection of the 'Norland' variety, which was released by the North Dakota Agricultural College (now North Dakota State University) in 1957. The darker red-skinned selection that became known as 'Red Norland' was identified and began distribution around 1965. The variety was specifically developed for northern climates and has become the standard red potato for variety trials in most North American seed potato certification programmes. Its success lies in its combination of early maturity, adaptation to short growing seasons, and consistent quality for fresh market sales.
