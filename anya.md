# Anya

## Summary
Anya is a second-early fingerling potato bred in Scotland in 1996 by crossing Pink Fir Apple and Désirée. A waxy salad potato with a distinctive nutty flavour, it holds its shape beautifully when cooked and has become a hallmark of British salad cookery. Named after Lady Sainsbury, it was developed in close collaboration with Sainsbury's supermarkets.

## Agronomic notes
Anya is a second-early variety, ready to harvest 15–17 weeks after planting under normal conditions. It produces prolific tuber numbers and has become popular for backyard container growing. The variety shows good resistance to common scab but is susceptible to several significant diseases: late blight, powdery scab, blackleg, and particularly vulnerable to eelworm (potato cyst nematode). Yields are moderate; the variety is grown more for flavour and culinary merit than production volume.

## Culinary notes
Anya's defining trait is its slightly nutty, almost chestnut-like flavour. The waxy white flesh holds its shape exceptionally well when boiled, steamed, or roasted, making it ideal for potato salads where structural integrity is important. It is excellent in warm salads, where its firm texture absorbs vinaigrettes without disintegrating. The variety can also be sautéed and roasted. Anya is particularly prized by chefs and experienced home cooks who value flavour and texture over yield.

## Origin & history
Anya was developed in 1996 at the Scottish Crop Research Institute (SCRI) in collaboration with Albert Bartlett and Sons (Airdrie) Ltd. The cross of Pink Fir Apple and Désirée was specifically developed with input from Sainsbury's supermarkets, and the variety was named in honour of Lady Sainsbury (the ballerina Anya Sainsbury). The patent rights held by SCRI expired in 2026. The variety became closely associated with Sainsbury's, where it was—and remains—a flagship salad potato offering in the UK market.
