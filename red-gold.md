# Red Gold

## Summary
Red Gold is a yellow-fleshed, red-skinned Canadian potato developed in the 1970s as a medium-sized, all-purpose variety. With short dormancy and high tuber set, it is prized for its buttery flavour and waxy texture, making it suitable for baking, boiling, roasting, and mashing. The variety offers moderate to high yields and good disease resistance to several viral pathogens.

## Agronomic notes
Red Gold matures in mid-season (typically 90–110 days) with a moderate to high yield depending on growing conditions. Tubers are medium-sized and oval to round in shape, with smooth, thin red skin and medium-depth eyes uniformly distributed over the surface. The variety exhibits short dormancy and high tuber set per plant, characteristics noted in breeding documentation. It grows best in well-drained, fertile soils with moderate rainfall or irrigation. Red Gold shows resistance to potato leafroll virus and potato virus Y, and is moderately resistant to common scab, though it is susceptible to potato virus A and potato virus S. The variety stores well in cool, dark conditions.

## Culinary notes
Red Gold's firm, waxy yellow flesh and buttery flavour make it highly versatile in the kitchen. The variety excels in applications where potatoes hold their shape — baking, boiling, roasting, and mashing are all recommended uses. The waxy texture and moderate starch content make it an all-purpose potato suitable for most cooking methods. It has acceptable chipping quality at harvest, though it is not primarily grown for processing into crisps/chips.

## Origin & history
Red Gold was developed in Canada in the 1970s through collaboration between Agriculture Canada, the University of Guelph, and the Ontario Ministry of Agriculture and Food. It was registered as a potato variety and has since been widely adopted in North America, particularly in the United States where it is also cultivated as a specialty or gourmet potato. The variety's short dormancy and balanced agronomic profile made it suitable for both home gardeners and commercial growers.
