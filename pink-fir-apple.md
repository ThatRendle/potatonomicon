# Pink Fir Apple

## Summary
Pink Fir Apple is a heritage fingerling potato variety originating from France and first imported to the United Kingdom in 1850. Distinguished by its long, distinctively knobbly tubers and dual-coloured pink and white skin, it is prized for its firm, creamy waxy flesh and rich nutty, earthy flavour. Primarily grown as a salad potato, it has become a specialty crop valued for its unique characteristics and ability to retain shape during cooking.

## Agronomic notes
Pink Fir Apple is a maincrop variety with medium-sized, elongated fingerling tubers that are famously difficult to peel due to their knobbly texture—typically boiled before peeling. The tubers have thin pink and white skin with cream to yellow waxy flesh inside. It exhibits high resistance to common scab but shows low resistance to late blight on both foliage and tubers, requiring vigilant disease monitoring and preventive measures such as crop rotation. The variety is susceptible to potato cyst nematodes (both Globodera rostochiensis Ro1 and Globodera pallida Pa2/3). Yield and dormancy characteristics are not well documented in commercial sources.

## Culinary notes
Pink Fir Apple is renowned for its culinary versatility and distinctive nutty, earthy flavour with subtle humic and loamy notes and hints of hazelnut. Its firm, waxy texture allows it to retain shape during cooking without becoming mushy, making it ideal for cold salads where it absorbs dressings beautifully. It is equally suitable for boiling, steaming, and roasting. The variety's creamy consistency and flavour profile have elevated it from a once-obscure heritage variety to a high-end specialty ingredient sought by chefs and home gardeners. The difficulty of peeling has not diminished its appeal; indeed, many preparations leave the thin skin intact.

## Origin & history
Pink Fir Apple is an old French variety that was first imported to the United Kingdom in 1850. The precise breeder is unknown; the variety predates most modern potato breeding records. In 1996, Pink Fir Apple was crossed with the Désirée variety to create the Anya potato, which was developed for Sainsbury's supermarkets in the UK. The variety has remained in continuous cultivation in the UK and has also become established in Australia, particularly Tasmania, where certified seed potatoes are commercially available.
