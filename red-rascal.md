# Red Rascal

## Summary
Red Rascal is a red-skinned, white-fleshed main-crop potato bred by New Zealand's Institute for Crop & Food Research and released in 1995. It is a versatile all-purpose cultivar prized for its high yield, full flavour, and strong disease resistance. Now widely cultivated in Australia and beyond, it is sought after by both home gardeners and professional chefs for its reliability and adaptability.

## Agronomic notes
Red Rascal matures in 90–100 days, making it a reliable main-crop variety. It yields high volumes of medium, oval tubers with very red skin. The variety demonstrates excellent agronomic traits: it has high resistance to both late blight (Phytophthora infestans) and powdery scab (Spongospora subterranea), reducing the need for fungicide applications. It stores well after harvest, which has contributed to its adoption across commercial and domestic growers in Australia and other regions.

## Culinary notes
Red Rascal's white flesh and floury texture make it an exceptionally versatile potato in the kitchen. It is equally at home boiled as a side dish, roasted until crispy, mashed into a creamy purée, baked whole, or deep-fried into chips. Its full flavour and waxy undertones allow it to complement both simple preparations with butter and herbs and more complex dishes. Chefs appreciate it for its consistency and ability to hold shape during cooking, while home cooks value its all-purpose nature that eliminates the need to stock multiple varieties.

## Origin & history
Red Rascal was developed by the New Zealand Institute for Crop & Food Research (now AgResearch) and released in 1995. It emerged during a period of intensified potato breeding in New Zealand aimed at producing modern varieties with improved disease resistance and yield. The cultivar's parentage remains undisclosed in public sources. Since its introduction, Red Rascal has become established in Australia, where it thrives in varied growing conditions and has become a fixture in both home gardens and commercial production.
