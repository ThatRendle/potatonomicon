# Russet Norkotah

## Summary
Russet Norkotah is an early-to-medium maturing, determinate potato variety released by North Dakota State University in 1987. It is now the second most popular potato variety in the United States for fresh-market use. Known for its attractive oval shape and consistent appearance, this variety produces low-to-medium specific gravity tubers with white flesh and shallow eyes that make it excellent for peeling.

## Agronomic notes
Russet Norkotah matures in 95–110 days from planting and is a determinate variety with rapid emergence, though it is sensitive to cold soil at planting. The medium-sized, upright vine produces about 85% row closure with light-to-medium tuber set (8–12 tubers per plant, set high on the hill). Growth abruptly stops at flowering, and the plant is hail-sensitive with little to no re-growth capacity, making it somewhat vulnerable to weather damage.

The variety has a small root system resulting in poor drought tolerance and a tendency to oversize under good growing conditions. Despite this, it has high yield potential, often exceeding 400 cwt/acre. Tubers are well-shaped long-ovals with medium dormancy (3–4 months), allowing storage until mid-March under typical commercial conditions. The variety is resistant to hollow heart and Columbia root-knot nematode, with medium resistance to common scab and silver scurf, but has low resistance to late blight and potato leafroll virus, requiring attention to disease management in some regions.

## Culinary notes
Russet Norkotah is primarily marketed as a fresh-market baker, with the count-carton trade as its main market. The tubers' white flesh and shallow, well-distributed eyes make them excellent for peeling. The variety is good for baking and roasting but performs poorly for chipping. Boiling is possible but not ideal — the tubers show poor-to-moderate performance with some after-cooking discoloration. With low-to-medium specific gravity (1.075–1.080) and medium starch content, the texture is suitable for general-purpose use.

## Origin & history
Russet Norkotah was released in 1987 by North Dakota State University. The variety was named for its Norkotah heritage — a reference to a group of russet potato strains originally developed in the Northern Great Plains. Since its release, it has become the second most popular potato variety in the United States for fresh-market use, with particular popularity among commercial growers. The consistent, attractive appearance and consistent oval shape of its tubers made it an immediate success in the fresh-market sector, particularly for count-carton retail packaging where uniformity is highly valued.
