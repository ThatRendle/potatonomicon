# Dutch Cream

## Summary
Dutch Cream is a long-season waxy potato with creamy yellow flesh, originally bred in the Netherlands in the 1880s from a cross of Kerr's Pink and Caleya. Introduced to Australia in 1959, it has become one of Australia's most popular potato varieties, prized for holding its shape during cooking and storing well. It is an all-purpose workhorse variety suitable for mashing, boiling, roasting, and salads.

## Agronomic notes
Dutch Cream is a late-season, indeterminate potato requiring 90–120 days to reach full maturity. As an indeterminate variety, it produces tubers along its growing stem throughout the season, requiring continuous mounding or coverage to encourage further development — growers are advised not to harvest early as the potatoes need extended time to develop fully underground. The variety is highly prolific and stores well, making it a popular commercial choice. It produces large-sized tubers with distinctive yellow skin and creamy white flesh with a yellow centre. While specific disease resistance data is limited, the variety has proven robust in Australian growing conditions over more than 60 years of cultivation.

## Culinary notes
Dutch Cream is a waxy potato that holds its shape exceptionally well when cooked, making it ideal for applications where potato integrity matters — boiling, salads, and mashing without mushiness. The creamy yellow flesh has a slightly sweet flavour with earthy undertones. The variety excels in both hot preparations (roasting, baking, mashing) and cold dishes (potato salads), and is praised for its buttery texture and flavour. The high proportion of waxy starch and low breakdown during cooking make it a true all-rounder in the kitchen.

## Origin & history
Dutch Cream was bred in the Netherlands in the 1880s as a cross between Kerr's Pink and a variety called Caleya. It spread throughout Europe before reaching Australia in the 1950s, officially introduced there in 1959. It has since become one of Australia's most iconic and commercially significant potato varieties, found in virtually every supermarket and greengrocer across the country. The long growing season and prolific harvest made it particularly well-suited to Australian growing conditions.
