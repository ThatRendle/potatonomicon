# Vivaldi

## Summary

Vivaldi is a modern second early potato from the Netherlands, bred by HZPC and widely cultivated in the UK and North America since its introduction in 2001. It is prized for its consistent oval shape, light yellow skin and flesh, and excellent creamy, buttery flavour. Named after Vivaldi to reflect its year-round availability across seasons and climates, this variety is beloved by home gardeners and commercial growers alike for its reliable yields and good storability.

## Agronomic notes

Vivaldi is a second early variety with relatively high tuber set and reliable yields. Tubers are notably uniform in size and shape, producing predominantly marketable product. The plants are tall with weakly pigmented stems and white flowers, providing good ground cover and rapid development during the growing season. Eye depth is shallow, making the tubers quick and easy to prepare in the kitchen. The variety has good storability characteristics, allowing extended shelf life when kept in cool conditions.

Disease resistance is a particular strength: Vivaldi is field immune to potato wart disease and highly resistant to both potato virus A and Y. It shows moderate resistance to leafroll virus, virus X, and late blight on tubers. However, growers should be aware it is moderately susceptible to late blight on leaves, powdery scab, rhizoctonia and skin spot. It is notably resistant to common scab, an important advantage for soil-prone areas. The variety produces a good crop with a longer-than-average harvest window.

## Culinary notes

Vivaldi's pale yellow flesh and creamy, buttery flavour make it a favourite for multiple cooking methods. The waxy texture holds shape well during boiling and is excellent in salads and cold potato dishes. The creamy nature also makes it superb for mashing and baking. Roasting brings out the buttery character. The variety earned recognition for its culinary quality, winning the Q Food and Drink awards in 2006 and gold at The Grocer Own Label Awards in 2011, where judges noted there was no need to add butter when preparing this variety. Home cooks and chefs value its versatility and consistent, appealing flavour.

## Origin & history

Vivaldi was bred by HZPC, the Dutch potato breeder, through a cross of the cultivars TZ 77 148 and Monalisa. Research and development took nine years before its introduction to market in 2001. The variety was subsequently passed to Naturally Best, a Lincolnshire-based wholesaler and promoter who distributed it widely in the UK market. (Note: Naturally Best was later acquired by Albert Bartlett & Sons in late 2006.)

The name Vivaldi is a deliberate reference to Antonio Vivaldi and his famous orchestral work "The Four Seasons," chosen because the potatoes are grown across multiple climates and countries, ensuring availability throughout all four seasons of the year. This naming reflects the variety's rapid adoption and widespread cultivation worldwide.
