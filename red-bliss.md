# Red Bliss

## Summary

Red Bliss is a distinctive waxy potato with bright red skin and white creamy flesh, introduced in the United States in 1940. Prized for its ability to hold shape during cooking and its versatile culinary applications, it has become a classic variety for potato salads, roasting, and other dishes where visual appeal and texture matter.

## Agronomic notes

Red Bliss matures in the early to second early season, making it suitable for both fresh market harvesting and early-season production. The variety is high-yielding under optimal conditions and produces medium-sized, round to oval tubers with shallow eyes. The smooth, thin, bright red skin is visually striking and remains intact during cooking. Red Bliss exhibits moderate resistance to common scab and adapts well to various climates, though it thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils. The variety stores well for extended periods when kept in cool, dry conditions. Many growers harvest Red Bliss young as new potatoes, taking advantage of their tender skins and creamy texture.

## Culinary notes

Red Bliss is a waxy potato with low starch content and moist flesh that holds its shape exceptionally well during cooking. This makes it ideal for potato salads, whether served warm or cold, as the tubers absorb dressings beautifully without becoming mushy. The variety excels in boiling, steaming, roasting, and gratins. The thin red skin is edible and requires no peeling, adding visual appeal and nutritional value to dishes. Red Bliss has a mild, slightly sweet flavour that pairs well with herbs such as rosemary, thyme, dill, and parsley. Although typically waxy potatoes are avoided for mashing, Red Bliss is sometimes mashed with the skin left on to create a denser, creamier texture. The variety is particularly popular in North American cuisine for its attractive presentation and culinary versatility.

## Origin & history

Red Bliss was introduced in the United States in 1940, though the exact breeder and parentage remain undocumented in widely available sources. The name references the heritage Bliss potato line, which was developed and promoted by B.K. Bliss & Sons, the influential 19th-century New York seed company. However, Red Bliss itself appears to be a distinct 20th-century introduction rather than a direct sport or selection of the earlier Bliss varieties. The variety quickly gained popularity as a fresh-market potato, valued for its attractive red skin, consistent quality, and reliable yields. It has become a staple in North American markets and home gardens.
