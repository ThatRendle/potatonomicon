# Wilja

## Summary
Wilja is a second early potato variety bred in the Netherlands in 1967 by Konst Research BV and released in 1975. It has become a favourite among home gardeners and potato enthusiasts in the United Kingdom, prized for its excellent all-purpose cooking qualities, reliable disease resistance, and distinctive earthy flavour that responds to growing conditions.

## Agronomic notes
Wilja produces good, consistent yields of large, uniform tubers even for a second early variety — an unusual trait that gives reliable harvests across plantings. The potatoes mature in approximately 15–17 weeks and are ready to harvest by July or August. They show excellent resistance to common scab and dry rot, along with good resistance to potato leafroll virus; the main weakness is susceptibility to potato cyst nematode (eelworm), which may limit suitability in affected soils. The variety also demonstrates good resistance to slug damage. The rough, russeted skin and brown-to-yellow colouring can appear less appealing than smoother commercial varieties, which partly explains why Wilja was dropped from supermarket shelves in favour of varieties like Estima, despite superior flavour.

## Culinary notes
Wilja is an exceptional all-rounder in the kitchen. The medium-dry texture and balanced starch content make it equally suited to boiling, roasting, baking in jackets, making chips, or using in salads. The potatoes hold their shape remarkably well when boiled, even if cooked slightly longer than necessary — a practical advantage for home cooking. The flesh is dense and pale yellow to cream in colour. Most notably, Wilja has a robust, distinctive earthy flavour that varies noticeably depending on the soil and growing conditions, which appeals to gardeners seeking potatoes with pronounced character rather than mild, neutral taste.

## Origin & history
Wilja was developed by Konst Research BV, a Dutch breeding programme, and is believed to be a cross between Climax potatoes and the breeding line KO 51-123. Created in 1967, the variety was released to the market in 1975. It has found particular success in the United Kingdom among home gardeners and specialty growers, where it is valued as a flavourful alternative to bland commercial varieties. Though it never achieved large-scale commercial cultivation due to its rough skin and lack of mass-market appeal, Wilja has established a dedicated niche, especially in Europe, where it remains a favourite among gardeners and food enthusiasts who prioritise taste and cooking performance over cosmetic appearance.
