# Toolangi Delight

## Summary
Toolangi Delight is a floury, all-purpose potato developed at the Victorian Government's research station in Toolangi, Australia. Prized for its white flesh with distinctive deep dimples, this potato breaks down beautifully when cooked, making it ideal for mashing, baking, and creating pillowy gnocchi. It has earned a reputation among Australian home gardeners for reliable disease-free performance and strong productivity.

## Agronomic notes
Toolangi Delight is a maincrop variety with reportedly high productivity. It produces oval tubers of medium size with white skin and characteristic deep dimples that give it a distinctive appearance. Growers note that the variety performs very well, with consistent disease-free results even in home gardens. The deep eye depth and floury nature suggest longer cooking times compared to waxy varieties. While specific dormancy data is not available, the variety's maincrop classification suggests medium-length dormancy typical of Australian floury potatoes.

## Culinary notes
This is a strongly floury potato with high starch content and low moisture. These characteristics make it ideal for applications where breakdown and fluffiness are desired: mashing produces exceptionally light and creamy results, and baking yields fluffy interiors. It is excellent for chips and fries, where the high starch contributes to crispness and yield. Gnocchi made from Toolangi Delight is renowned for its texture. The potato's excellent flavour is noted in sources. The floury texture means it is less suitable for boiling in salads, as tubers may break apart during cooking.

## Origin & history
Toolangi Delight was developed by the Victorian Government's agricultural research station located at Toolangi in Victoria, Australia. It represents mid-20th-century Australian potato breeding efforts aimed at creating locally-adapted varieties. The name derives from the Toolangi location where it was developed. The variety has become established particularly in eastern Australia, though quarantine regulations have limited its distribution to some regions (notably Queensland).
