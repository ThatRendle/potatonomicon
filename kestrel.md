# Kestrel

## Summary

Kestrel is a quick-maturing early potato with high yields, developed in northern Scotland in 1992 by renowned breeder Dr. Jack Dunnett. Famous for its distinctive light cream skin marked with shallow purple eyes and brown spots, it is now widely grown across the United Kingdom, Australia, and New Zealand. Prized for its versatility in the kitchen, firm texture, and rich, earthy flavour, Kestrel has become one of the most recognizable potato varieties in the fresh market, particularly popular with both home and commercial growers.

## Agronomic notes

Kestrel is an early-maturing variety with a medium dormancy period, making it reliable for commercial cultivation. It achieves high yields and produces medium to large, uniformly oval tubers with a characteristic pale cream skin. The shallow eyes are distinctively marked with purple colouring and brown spotting, making Kestrel arguably the most identifiable variety in the Australian fresh market. The variety demonstrates good resistance to damage and bruising, and moderate bruising resistance overall, contributing to its appeal for commercial growers. Tubers have thin, smooth skin that is easily handled and stores reasonably well.

## Culinary notes

Kestrel is an all-purpose potato with firm, crisp, cream-coloured flesh that cooks to a smooth texture. When cooked, it develops a rich, earthy, and buttery flavour. The variety is exceptionally versatile, excelling at roasting, boiling, mashing, and frying. It produces excellent chips and French fries, and can be cooked with or without the skin. When mashed, it yields a creamy consistency with good structural integrity. This culinary flexibility, combined with its attractive appearance, makes it a favourite with both home cooks and professional chefs.

## Origin & history

Kestrel was developed in 1992 by Dr. Jack Dunnett, a renowned Scottish potato breeder based at Caithness Potatoes in northern Scotland. The variety was bred from a cross between the Cara potato and an unnamed variety of Solanum vernei, incorporating the naturally occurring purple eye trait from its Cara parentage. From its Scottish origins, Kestrel spread globally and became particularly established in the United Kingdom, Australia, and New Zealand. The variety remains a firm favourite among gardeners and commercial growers worldwide, and Kestrel is one of Dr. Dunnett's most enduring commercial successes—part of a legacy that includes Valor, Swift, Winston, Argos, Harmony, and Osprey.
