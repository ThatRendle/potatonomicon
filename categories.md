# Potato Groups

63 varieties organized into six primary groups by their most visually distinctive characteristic — skin colour or, for fingerlings, shape. Each variety appears in exactly one primary group. A secondary index by culinary texture follows.

---

## Russet

Thick, rough-netted brown or golden-russet skin; white flesh; high starch. The dominant commercial type in the United States; archetypal baking and frying potato.

| Variety | Country | Texture |  Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Russet Burbank | US / AU | floury | The world's most planted potato |
| Ranger Russet | US | floury | Long, uniform tubers; fry specialist |
| Russet Norkotah | US | all-purpose | Lighter starch than most russets |
| Umatilla Russet | US | floury | High-yield commercial standard |
| Goldrush Russet | US | all-purpose | Disease-resistant; good fresh-market |
| Golden Wonder | UK | floury | Golden-russet skin; Scottish heritage; gave its name to a crisp brand |

## Fingerling

Elongated, finger-like shape — typically 3–5 inches long and narrow. Most are waxy with rich, nutty, or buttery flavour. Fingerlings span all colour groups; skin colours within this group range from golden and rose-pink to purple.

| Variety | Country | Texture | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Banana Fingerling | US | waxy | Golden skin, yellow flesh; banana-crescent shape |
| French Fingerling | US | waxy | Rose-pink skin, golden yellow flesh; medium-small |
| Rose Finn Apple | US | waxy | Deep rose-pink skin, yellow flesh; very slender |
| LaRatte | US / FR | waxy | French heirloom; nutty chestnut flavour; chef favourite |
| Russian Banana | US | waxy | Old Baltic heirloom; golden, waxy, dense |
| Pink Fir Apple | UK / AU | waxy | Victorian heirloom; knobbly, irregular; earthy flavour |
| Anya | UK | waxy | Piper × Désirée cross; knobbly pinkish-tan skin |
| Kipfler | AU | waxy | Austrian-origin fingerling; tan skin, yellow flesh |
| Jersey Royal | UK | waxy | PDO variety; kidney-finger shape; grown only in Jersey |

## Index: By Culinary Texture

### Floury
High starch. Best for baking, mashing, chips / fries. Breaks down when boiled.

Russet Burbank · Ranger Russet · Umatilla Russet · Golden Wonder · Kennebec · Pentland Dell · King Edward · Lady Balfour · Adirondack Blue · All Blue

### All-purpose
Medium starch. Versatile — bake, mash, boil, roast.

Goldrush Russet · Russet Norkotah · Sebago · Coliban · Maris Piper · Kerr's Pink · Yukon Gold · Yukon Gem · Keuka Gold · Wilja · Sante · Fontane · Spunta · Golden Delight · Red Gold · Adirondack Red · Rooster · Desiree · Mozart · Otway Red · Red Rascal · Ruby Lou · Toolangi Delight

### Waxy
Low starch. Best for salads, boiling, gratins. Holds shape when cooked.
