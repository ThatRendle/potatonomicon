# Patrone

## Summary
Patrone is a waxy Dutch potato variety with pale yellow skin and creamy yellow flesh. Originally bred in the Netherlands, it is cultivated in Australia and prized for salad applications where its firm, waxy texture holds shape through cooking. It is a versatile potato well-suited to boiling, roasting, and traditional potato salad preparations.

## Agronomic notes
Patrone is classified as a waxy potato variety, meaning it contains more moisture and sugar relative to starch compared to floury varieties. It typically produces small to medium tubers with a smooth skin. The variety is valued for its consistent texture and ability to retain its shape during cooking, making it reliable for culinary applications where structural integrity matters. Specific information about yield, maturity class, and disease resistance profiles is not widely documented in available sources.

## Culinary notes
Patrone's waxy texture and creamy, firm flesh make it ideal for potato salads, where it resists breaking apart when dressed and mixed. Its low starch content means it is not recommended for mashing—it will produce a chunky, undesirable mash—or for baking and deep frying, where starchy varieties perform better. Patrone excels when boiled whole or sliced, roasted, or used in casseroles and soups. Its delicate, mild flavour complements light dressings and fresh preparations typical of Dutch and Australian cuisine.

## Origin & history
Patrone was bred by König GH van Haeringen Erven, a Dutch potato breeding operation. Its pedigree combines two generations of crosses: (Bintje x Record) x (Black 581 x Alpha), drawing on well-established Dutch potato genetics. The variety represents the Dutch potato breeding tradition's focus on waxy, all-purpose salad varieties. While the exact year of introduction is unknown, Patrone became established in Australian cultivation where it is listed as a standard waxy potato variety.
