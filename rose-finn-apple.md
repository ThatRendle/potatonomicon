# Rose Finn Apple

## Summary
Rose Finn Apple is an heirloom fingerling potato with rose-pink skin and golden-yellow flesh, prized for its waxy texture and rich, nutty flavour. First documented in cultivation during the 1840s, it remains a specialty variety favoured by home gardeners and chefs for roasting and salads. The small, knobbly fingerling tubers hold their shape well when cooked, making it excellent for dishes requiring textural integrity.

## Agronomic notes
Rose Finn Apple is an early maincrop variety maturing in 105–135 days. It produces moderate yields with a characteristic heavy tuber set, meaning many smaller tubers rather than a few large ones. Tubers are small, knobbly fingerlings with light red to rose-pink skin and golden flesh. The variety exhibits good scab resistance and medium late blight tolerance. It prefers hilling and steady watering; excessive knobbiness can occur if watering is inconsistent. The variety has a medium dormancy period and grows vigorously with a large plant habit.

## Culinary notes
Rose Finn Apple's waxy texture and firm, moderately dry flesh make it excellent for boiling, baking, and roasting — applications where potatoes must hold their shape. The nutty, rich flavour is particularly prized in potato salads and as a roasted side. Its moist, buttery flesh resists breaking apart during cooking, making it ideal for gourmet preparations. The waxy nature also suits braising and sautéing; it is less suited to mashing due to its texture and starch content.

## Origin & history
Rose Finn Apple is documented as a century heirloom grown since the 1840s. Neither the exact country of origin, breeder, nor parentage are recorded in widely available sources. The variety's name and characteristics suggest historical cultivation in North America, possibly as part of 19th-century immigrant potato heritage, though specific documentation remains elusive. It has persisted as a specialty variety maintained by seed savers, organic growers, and heirloom enthusiasts.
