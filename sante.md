# Sante

## Summary
Sante is an early maincrop potato bred in the Netherlands in 1971 and introduced to commercial cultivation in 1983. Known for its yellow tubers, high yield, and excellent disease resistance profile, Sante has become widely grown across Europe, North America, and beyond. It is prized in both fresh and processing markets for its floury texture and firm flesh that resists discolouration during cooking.

## Agronomic notes
Sante is a vigorous, high-yielding early maincrop variety with tall, spreading growth and thick stems. Plants exhibit very good foliage development, though initially slow to establish. The variety grows well even with below-average nitrogen fertilisation. Large, uniform, oval yellow tubers with light yellow flesh are produced in high numbers. Eyes are medium to shallow, reducing peeling waste. Medium dormancy allows reasonable storage without premature sprouting. Notably resistant to potato cyst nematodes (both golden nematode pathotypes Ro1/4 and field resistance to Ro2/3, Ro4 and white nematode Pa2) and wart disease; resistance to powdery scab is slight to not susceptible. Excellent virus resistance and good resistance to internal bruising are significant agronomic advantages. The variety is moderately sensitive to drought stress.

## Culinary notes
Sante's rather firm to floury flesh makes it versatile across cooking methods. It has medium-high dry matter content (approximately 18–20%), contributing to a satisfying, fluffy texture when cooked. The flesh resists discolouration after cooking, making it ideal for fresh market sales and processing applications. It excels in both boiling and frying, with low sugar content helping minimise acrylamide formation during processing into chips and fries. The variety is well suited to export markets due to its uniform tuber size and reliable quality.

## Origin & history
Sante was bred by J. Vegter from a cross of SVP Y 66-13-636 × SVP AM 66-42 in Veendam, Netherlands, in 1971, under Agrico's potato breeding programme. It was formally introduced to commercial cultivation in 1983 and quickly gained acceptance across European markets. The variety was registered in Canada in 1997 and in Russia in 1993, and is now grown commercially in the United Kingdom, United States, Australia, and numerous other countries. Its combination of disease resistance and processing quality has made it one of the most widely cultivated varieties globally.
