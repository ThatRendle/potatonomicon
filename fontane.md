# Fontane

## Summary
Fontane is a high-yielding early maincrop potato variety developed by Agrico in the Netherlands in 1986. Bred from Agria and AR 76-34-3, it is the leading processing variety across Europe, prized for French fries, flakes, and crisps production. The variety combines large, uniform tubers with high dry matter content and low sugar levels, making it ideal for industrial processing with minimal acrylamide formation.

## Agronomic notes
Fontane exhibits robust agronomic performance with rapid initial plant development and sturdy growth habit. It produces large, uniform tubers in an oval to long-oval shape with shallow eyes. The variety yields high volumes and is adaptable to different soil types, though it benefits from nitrogen fertilization above standard recommendations (approximately +30% additional nitrogen). Tuber number is high and consistent. The variety has long dormancy, which supports extended storage while maintaining low sugar content—a key advantage for processing quality. Fontane is moderately susceptible to late blight on foliage and requires preventative spraying protocols until full haulm death. It shows resistance to golden nematode Ro 1/4 but is susceptible to other pests and diseases, including slightly elevated susceptibility to common scab and powdery scab.

## Culinary notes
Fontane is a dedicated processing potato, not typically chosen for table use. Its high dry matter content (18-21% starch) and low sugar levels make it exceptional for French fries, where it develops minimal acrylamide levels during frying—a significant food safety advantage in commercial processing. It is equally well-suited to flake and crisp production. The pale yellow flesh and high starch content deliver the texture and colour consistency demanded by industrial food manufacturers. While suitable for all-purpose applications, Fontane is overwhelmingly grown and marketed for processing rather than fresh consumption.

## Origin & history
Fontane was created at Agrico's research facilities in 1986 from a cross between Agria and AR 76-34-3. Agrico, founded in 1973 as a cooperative merger in the Netherlands, has become the world's leading breeder and supplier of seed potatoes, and Fontane exemplifies the variety's commitment to processing excellence. The variety rapidly became the leading potato for processing across Europe and is now cultivated worldwide. Its development reflects the post-1980s shift in potato breeding toward specialized processing cultivars with consistent quality parameters for industrial manufacture.
