# Jazzy

## Summary
Jazzy is a modern second early salad potato developed in the Netherlands and first released commercially in the UK in 2013. It produces abundant small, cream-skinned tubers with light yellow flesh and a distinctive waxy texture. The variety has won the RHS Award of Garden Merit and is prized for its ability to hold shape in salads and its pleasant, subtly sweet flavour.

## Agronomic notes
Jazzy matures as a second early variety, ready to harvest approximately 14 weeks after planting under good conditions. It produces a very high number of tubers per plant, though individual tubers are small to below-average in size, making them ideal for whole boiling and salads. The variety exhibits very short dormancy, allowing for extended harvesting or storage in the ground with continued palatability. Plants are slightly shorter than average, which provides some advantage in windy conditions. Jazzy grows well in most soil types and is flexible for various cultivation conditions. The tubers feature shallow eyes and very smooth skin with a thin cream or white coat that requires no peeling for fresh salads.

## Culinary notes
Jazzy is a salad specialist due to its naturally waxy texture, which allows tubers to hold their shape when boiled or dressed. The potatoes produce a uniform small size, making them well-suited to whole boiling without splitting. The flesh has a slightly sweet and creamy flavour. They excel in warm and cold salads, where their ability to absorb dressings without becoming mushy is highly valued. They can also be roasted whole to advantage. The thin, smooth cream skin requires no peeling when used fresh for salads; however, peeling is necessary if mashing is desired. Under storage, the skins gradually harden slightly over several months, after which peeling becomes necessary.

## Origin & history
Jazzy was developed by Meijer, a Dutch potato breeder, and first received the RHS Award of Garden Merit in 2013 before being launched commercially in the UK in 2014. The variety is a cross between Franceline and Cupido. Its name reflects its modern, lively character in the salad potato market. The variety revolutionized the UK salad potato sector with its combination of high yield, excellent texture, and minimal preparation requirements—no peeling for fresh use.
