# Sebago

## Summary
Sebago is a late-maincrop all-purpose potato developed in Maine in 1938 by the USDA and Maine Agricultural Experiment Station as a cross between Chippewa and Katahdin. Originally bred for late blight resistance, it arrived in Australia in 1940 and became one of the country's most popular varieties. The potato is prized for its versatility in the kitchen and reliable disease resistance.

## Agronomic notes
Sebago is a late-maincrop variety that yields moderate tuber production with consistent oval to elliptical shape. The tubers are medium in size with light yellow-brown semi-smooth skin featuring dark brown spots and shallow eyes that are easy to peel. Flesh is dense, firm, and white to cream-coloured. The variety matures reliably with medium dormancy, making it suitable for extended storage. Sebago demonstrates moderate to good resistance to several key potato diseases including late blight, common scab, fusarium dry rot, early blight, tuber net necrosis, verticillium wilt, and PVY, making it a reliable choice for growers seeking disease-resistant varieties.

## Culinary notes
Sebago is an excellent all-purpose potato prized for its versatility in the kitchen. When cooked, the tubers are fluffy with a mild, earthy taste thanks to their medium starch content. The potato works well across a range of cooking methods including boiling, mashing, and general cooking applications. It is particularly popular in Australia where it is regarded as the most popular all-purpose tuber and is used widely in supermarkets and home kitchens alike. The uniform shape and consistent texture make it suitable as a substitute for other varieties in most recipes.

## Origin & history
Sebago was developed in 1938 by the United States Department of Agriculture in partnership with the Maine Agricultural Experiment Station. The variety was created specifically for its resistance to late blight, a devastating fungal disease that had long plagued potato crops. The parents are Chippewa and Katahdin, two important US potato varieties of that era. Following its release in the United States, Sebago potatoes were introduced to Victoria, Australia in 1940, where they took root and flourished. Today, despite its origins in Maine, Sebago is one of Australia's most iconic and widely grown potato varieties, far more popular there than in its native North America. The variety is also cultivated in Canada and select regions of the United States.
