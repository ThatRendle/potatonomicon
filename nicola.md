# Nicola

## Summary
Nicola is a second-early, yellow-fleshed waxy potato variety developed in Germany in 1973 by the plant breeder Hans-Juergen Fitschen of Europlant. Prized for its uniform, smooth-skinned oval tubers and consistent high yields, it has become a popular salad and boiling potato across Europe and Australia. Its waxy texture and creamy flavour make it a kitchen favourite for fresh dishes.

## Agronomic notes
Nicola matures in the second-early season, typically requiring 15–16 weeks from planting to harvest. Plants produce abundant medium-sized tubers with a fine, smooth skin and shallow eyes. The variety exhibits high and consistent yields, with tubers forming uniformly on the plant. It demonstrates good resistance to potato cyst nematode (eelworm) and common scab, making it a reliable choice for organic and conventional growers. Medium dormancy allows for reasonable storage without premature sprouting.

## Culinary notes
Nicola's waxy, low-starch composition gives it a firm, fine-grained texture that holds shape when cooked—ideal for boiling, steaming, and cold salads. The pale yellow flesh cooks to a buttery, creamy consistency with a mild, nutty, earthy flavour. It is equally suited to mashing, gnocchi-making, and roasting, though it shines in applications where structural integrity matters: potato salads, gratins, and whole-potato dishes.

## Origin & history
Nicola was bred in 1973 by Hans-Juergen Fitschen at Europlant, a plant breeding company in Lüneburg, Germany. It arose from a cross between Hansa and Danae potato varieties. Following its official registration in Germany, Dutch farmers quickly adopted Nicola for commercial cultivation in the Netherlands, where it has remained a standard variety across 3,500+ hectares. The variety later spread throughout the United Kingdom and into Australia, where it is now a recognized staple. In 2016, Nicola was named "Potato of the Year" in Germany, recognizing its quality and consistency.
