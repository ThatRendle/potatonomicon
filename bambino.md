# Bambino

## Summary
Bambino is a modern UK maincrop salad potato with excellent disease resistance and good yields. It produces small, round/oval tubers with white skin and cream flesh, bred by Cygnet PB. It is widely approved by major UK supermarkets and valued for its waxy texture and positive eating quality.

## Agronomic notes
Bambino is a maincrop potato that can also be planted as a second early for smaller harvest. It produces high yields with consistently good skin finish. The variety demonstrates unusual Ro1 resistance compared to other salad varieties, and has excellent resistance to powdery and common scab, as well as good resistance to gangrene, dry rot, and blackleg, giving it good storability with long dormancy. This makes it well-suited to storage and later commercial distribution. Ready to harvest between August and October.

## Culinary notes
Bambino is an excellent-flavoured salad potato with a waxy, creamy texture that is ideal for boiling and baking. It maintains its structure during cooking and does not disintegrate, making it particularly reliable for salads and warm potato dishes. All consumer taste tests have been very positive, and Bambino is now approved by all major UK supermarkets, establishing it as a trusted retail variety.

## Origin & history
Bambino was bred by Cygnet PB, a UK potato breeding programme. It results from the cross Navan × Boxer. The variety has achieved rapid adoption in the UK market, particularly through major supermarket chains, establishing itself as a standard salad potato for home growers and commercial producers alike.
