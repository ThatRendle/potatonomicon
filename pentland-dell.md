# Pentland Dell

## Summary

Pentland Dell is a Scottish-bred maincrop potato introduced in 1961, renowned for its large long-oval tubers with white skin and creamy flesh. Widely grown for commercial processing, particularly for chips and crisps, it combines high yields with strong disease resistance and versatile culinary applications.

## Agronomic notes

Pentland Dell is a maincrop variety requiring approximately 20 weeks from planting (mid to late April) to harvest (August onwards). The plants exhibit good growth habit suitable for commercial production. The variety produces consistently large, long-oval shaped tubers with very shallow eyes—a desirable trait for mechanical harvesting and ease of peeling. The shallow eye depth minimises waste during preparation.

Yield performance is notably high, making it a favourite for commercial growers and processors. Disease resistance is exceptionally strong across common potato diseases, and the variety shows good resistance to slugs, which reduces pest management requirements. The firm and dry flesh provides excellent storage characteristics, allowing for extended storage at cool temperatures after harvest.

## Culinary notes

Pentland Dell is prized for its floury texture and high dry matter content, making it ideal for baking, roasting, and particularly for chipping and crisp production. The firm, dry flesh holds its shape well during cooking, though it has a tendency to disintegrate during boiling, limiting its use in salads or boiled potato dishes. Home gardeners value it for homemade chips and crispy baked potatoes, while the food industry values it as a processor's favourite for industrial crisps and chip production. The long shape is particularly suited to cut fries and hand chips.

## Origin & history

Pentland Dell was bred at the Scottish Plant Breeding Station, resulting from a cross between Roslin Chania and Roslin Sasumua. It was introduced in 1961 as part of the station's ambitious breeding programme. The variety was the second to bear the Pentland name from the breeding station, following the earlier Pentland Crown. By 2007, Pentland Dell remained the seventh most widely grown potato cultivar in Britain, testament to its enduring commercial value and reliability. The variety continues to be supplied as Northern Ireland Certified Seed Potatoes, ensuring quality and varietal purity for growers.
