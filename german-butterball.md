# German Butterball

## Summary
German Butterball is a late-season heirloom potato with a distinctively rich, buttery flavour and creamy texture. Introduced by David Ronniger in 1988, it is prized for its excellent storage life and its ability to hold shape during cooking, making it ideal for potato salads and other applications where structural integrity matters.

## Agronomic notes
German Butterball is a late-maturing variety, requiring 110–135 days to reach full harvest size. It produces medium-sized tubers with a smooth, golden skin marked by fine netting and shallow eyes. The vines are vigorous and plants should be managed for good skin set before harvest. The variety has excellent storage longevity and maintains quality well under proper storage conditions.

## Culinary notes
This potato has become a favourite among cooks and gardeners for its exceptional flavour and texture. The butter-yellow flesh is dense and creamy with a rich, almost custard-like consistency when cooked. Unlike many waxy varieties that can become grainy, German Butterball remains tender and holds together exceptionally well, making it the first choice for potato salads where tuber integrity is essential. It is also excellent for hash browns, steamed dishes, and baked potatoes. The buttery flavour develops fully when cooked gently.

## Origin & history
German Butterball was first introduced by David Ronniger in 1988. Ronniger, a farmer and potato specialist based in Moyie Springs, Idaho, cultivated over two hundred potato varieties, many of them heirloom cultivars originally popular in pre-19th-century Europe. The German Butterball represents Ronniger's passion for preserving and promoting forgotten European potato traditions. Today it is widely available across the United States through specialty producers, farmers markets, and home gardeners, and remains a benchmark heirloom potato for its distinctive buttery character.
