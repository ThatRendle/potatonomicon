# Mozart

## Summary
Mozart is a Dutch-bred maincrop potato introduced by HZPC in 2003, notable for its attractive appearance with light pink skin and pale yellow flesh. It is prized for uniform tuber size and excellent cooking quality, particularly in mashing, roasting, and baking applications. Mozart is increasingly popular in Australia as a replacement for Desiree, offering similar culinary characteristics with more uniform shape.

## Agronomic notes
Mozart is a medium-late maturity potato with moderate-to-high yields and large, oval tubers. It shows good bruising tolerance and excellent storability. The variety demonstrates good resistance to common scab but is susceptible to late blight and blackleg. It has medium dormancy and relatively shallow eyes, reducing peeling waste. The variety produces 9–11 tubers per plant with consistent sizing, making it attractive for commercial and home cultivation alike.

## Culinary notes
Mozart is a versatile all-purpose potato (cooking type AB, slightly firm) with 20.3% dry matter and 14.4% starch content. It holds its shape well when cooked, making it ideal for mashing, roasting, and baking while also performing well in chip/fries applications. The flesh remains creamy and pale yellow after cooking. Its waxy-to-medium texture and good bruising tolerance mean tubers arrive at the table in excellent condition. Cooks favour it for both everyday and traditional dishes where potato integrity is desired.

## Origin & history
Mozart was developed and introduced by HZPC (Holland Potato Company), a major Dutch potato breeding and seed provider, in 2003. The variety quickly gained acceptance across Europe and Australia, where it is now widely cultivated as a fresh-market and processing variety. The variety's uniform appearance and culinary quality have made it a popular choice for growers seeking to replace traditional maincrop varieties. Its parentage remains proprietary to HZPC.
