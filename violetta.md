# Violetta

## Summary
Violetta is a striking specialist heritage potato variety with deep indigo blue skin and matching blue flesh throughout. Bred in Germany by Karsten Ellenberg and added to the UK National List in 2012, it produces long oval tubers and is prized for its dramatic appearance and sweet, delicate flavour in savoury dishes. The variety is best enjoyed with the skin on to retain its distinctive colour.

## Agronomic notes
Violetta is an early maincrop variety with medium yields and a shallow-eyed tuber form. The plants exhibit medium to tall growth habit with distinctive blue-violet flowers. The variety demonstrates good resistance to dry rot (Fusarium sulphureum) and moderate resistance to leafroll virus, bruising, and splitting. However, it is susceptible to tuber late blight and to potato cyst nematodes, particularly Globodera rostochiensis and Globodera pallida. Despite these vulnerabilities, growers have reported good production, though the dark blue skin makes harvesting by hand challenging—tubers are difficult to spot in soil and require careful finger-work to locate without damage.

## Culinary notes
Violetta has a slightly fluffy, waxy texture that holds its shape well when cooked, making it excellent for salads, boiling, steaming, and sautéing. The potatoes can also be roasted or fried into chips. Its delicate, sweet flavour works particularly well in savoury dishes. The defining feature is the blue flesh, which persists through cooking and creates striking visual presentation. Cooks are advised to retain the skin during preparation to preserve the characteristic blue colour and add visual appeal to the finished dish. The variety has gained popularity in specialty markets and with chefs seeking distinctive, visually striking potatoes.

## Origin & history
Violetta originated in Germany, bred by the renowned potato breeder Karsten Ellenberg in the early 2000s. The variety was formally added to the UK National List in 2012 and has since become established in both the United Kingdom and Ireland, where seed growers have actively promoted it to producers and home gardeners. Its unique purple-blue coloration and heritage character have made it a favourite for specialty potato cultivation and culinary presentation.
