# Nadine

## Summary
Nadine is a second early potato variety bred in 1987 by the renowned UK potato breeder Jack Dunnett. Known for producing very high yields of attractive, uniform white-skinned tubers with shallow eyes, it has become one of the most successful commercial varieties in the UK. Its waxy texture and mild flavour make it exceptionally versatile for both fresh market and processing applications.

## Agronomic notes
Nadine matures as a second early, producing a very high yield of bright, marketable tubers well-suited to pre-packing. The tubers are medium-sized, oval to round, with smooth white to cream skin and shallow eyes that make peeling easier. Under favourable conditions, Nadine is noted as an indisputably high-yielding variety. It shows good resistance to common scab and to potato cyst nematode (Ro1 strain), with moderate resistance to late blight. However, it is susceptible to dry rot (Fusarium sulphureum), powdery scab, blackleg, and potato leafroll virus. The variety is reliable and easy to grow and store, making it popular with commercial growers across temperate and warmer climates.

## Culinary notes
Nadine is a waxy potato with white to cream flesh and a mild, slightly sweet flavour. Its firm texture and shape-retention make it ideal for boiling and salads, where it absorbs dressings beautifully without disintegrating. The RHS notes it is particularly suitable for baking and is also suitable for chipping. Its versatility across these applications has made it a favoured choice for both home cooks and commercial food processors. The waxy nature means it is not ideal for mashing, as the texture can become gluey.

## Origin & history
Nadine was introduced around 1987 by the famous British potato breeder Jack Dunnett, whose work significantly shaped modern UK potato varieties. The variety is held by Caithness Potato Breeders Ltd. Its commercial success has been remarkable—under favourable conditions it is described as "indisputably the highest yielding variety in the UK," and it has proven its versatility across North Temperate and Warm Southern climates, demonstrating exceptional adaptation to varied growing conditions. The variety has become a cornerstone of UK potato production for both fresh market and processing.
