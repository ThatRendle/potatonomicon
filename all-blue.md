# All Blue

## Summary
All Blue is a heirloom blue potato variety with distinctive blue skin and flesh, punctuated by a white inner ring. Suspected to originate in Scotland or the United States in the late 19th century, it has become a specialty or novelty market variety. Known for excellent storage, good drought tolerance, and higher starch content than other blue potatoes, it is a multipurpose tuber suited to many cooking methods.

## Agronomic notes
All Blue is a late-season maturing variety with vigorous, tall, semi-erect plants. Tubers are oblong and medium-sized, typically 5–6 inches in length, with yields generally moderate. The plants produce uniform tubers with blue-purple stems, dark green leaves, and distinctive blue flowers with blue-purple stripes. The skin is deep blue, and the flesh is predominantly blue throughout, though a white or pale ring appears just inside the skin—a diagnostic feature of the variety. This white ring is particularly visible when the tuber is cut. The variety exhibits intermediate eye depth. Dormancy is short, meaning sprouts emerge relatively quickly after storage. All Blue has good resistance to common scab and is notably drought tolerant; however, it performs poorly in very wet or waterlogged conditions. The variety has been widely used in breeding programs, particularly for developing new varieties like Bora Valley and Purple Majesty, due to its male fertility and self-compatibility.

## Culinary notes
All Blue is multipurpose and works well boiled, mashed, sautéed, baked, or fried. Its higher starch content and floury texture make it particularly suitable for making naturally blue mashed potatoes with vibrant color. When cooked, the blue flesh pales slightly but retains its distinctive hue. The tuber stores excellently, maintaining quality over extended periods. The flavour is reported as mild and subdued compared to other blue potato varieties, making it a choice more prized for novelty and appearance than for exceptional taste. In modern specialty markets, it is valued as a visual centerpiece in cooking and plating.

## Origin & history
All Blue is a heirloom potato variety of unknown exact origin, though it is suspected to have originated either in Scotland (UK) or the United States in the late 19th century. The variety has been documented for over 100 years and has accumulated several synonymous names throughout its history, including "Blue Marker," "Fenton Blue," and "River John Blue." It was originally used in agricultural settings as a marker potato to demarcate field boundaries and separate potato varieties during cultivation. Blue potatoes like All Blue are believed to be among the most direct descendants of potatoes domesticated nearly 10,000 years ago in the high Andes Mountains of Peru, where selective cultivation developed their deep blue pigmentation due to a high concentration of the antioxidant anthocyanin.
