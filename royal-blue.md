# Royal Blue

## Summary
Royal Blue is an Australian potato variety prized for its striking purple skin and yellow flesh. This all-purpose waxy potato has earned popularity in Australian kitchens for its versatility, performing equally well in roasts, mashes, chips, and salads. It is widely available in Australian supermarkets and maintains year-round cultivation.

## Agronomic notes
Royal Blue demonstrates a year-round growing season in Australia, indicating strong adaptability to local conditions. The variety produces medium-sized, oval tubers with consistent characteristics. It exhibits a waxy potato phenotype with low starch content and high moisture, contributing to its firm texture that holds shape well during cooking without breaking apart. The potato shows moderate yield performance and medium eye depth typical of waxy potato types.

## Culinary notes
Royal Blue's waxy texture and low starch content make it an all-purpose potato that excels across multiple cooking methods. It is particularly valued for roasting, where it caramelises beautifully while maintaining its structure. The potato performs exceptionally well when boiled or steamed for salads, in creamy mashed potato preparations, and as chips or fries. Its golden, buttery flesh has a slightly sweet and nutty flavour profile that distinguishes it from other waxy varieties. Many Australian cooks favour it for gnocchi and other specialty preparations.

## Origin & history
Royal Blue is an Australian cultivar that has become one of the country's popular coloured potato varieties. It represents the wave of blue and purple potatoes that gained traction in Australian home and commercial gardening. The exact year of introduction and breeding lineage remain unrecorded in publicly available sources. The variety is commercially available as certified seed potato and is produced by several Australian growers including premium producers such as Mitolo Family Farms.
